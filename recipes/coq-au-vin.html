Anthony Bourdain's Coq au vin

Description

Like many French dishes, Coq au vin looks difficult to make. The reality, however, is that it is very simple. You could spend a decent afternoon making it, with plenty of opportunity for breaks. This recipe comes from chef, author, television host, and all-around badass, Anthony Bourdain and is featured in his Congo episodes of Parts Unkown.

Ingredients

  • One bottle plus One cup of red wine
  • One onion, diced
  • One carrot, cut into 1/4-inch slices
  • One celery rib, cut into 1/2-inch slices
  • Four whole cloves
  • One tablespoon whole black peppercorns
  • One bundle of aromatic herbs (bouquet garni)
  • one whole chicken, trimmed
  • Salt and freshly ground pepper
  • Two tablespoons olive oil
  • Six tablespoons butter, softened
  • One tablespoon flour
  • 1/4 lb slab or country bacon, cut into small oblongs (lardons) about 1/4x1 inch
  • 1/2 lb small, white button mushrooms, stems removed
  • 12 pearl onions, peeled
  • Pinch of sugar

Steps

Day Before

Combine the bottle of red wine, diced onions, sliced carrots, celery, clover, peppercorns, and bouquet garni in a large, deep bowl. Add the chicken, and make sure the chicken is fully submerged. Cover the bowl wiht plastic wrap, and refrigerate overnight.

Day Of

  1. Remove chicken from marinade, pat dry, and set aside
  2. Strain marinade through a fine strainer, reserving the liquids and solids seperately
  3. Season chicken with salt and pepper inside and out
  4. Heat oil and two tablespoons of butter in a large Dutch oven until almost smoking
  5. Sear chicken, turning with tongs to brown the skin
  6. Once browned, remove the chicken from the pot and set aside
  7. Add reserved onions, celery, and carrot to the pot
  8. Cook over medium-high heat, stirring occasionally until vegetables are soft and golden brown
  9. Sprinkle flour over vegetables and mix well with wooden spoon
  10. Stir in reserved strained marinade
  11. Return chicken to the pot along with bouquet garni
  12. Cook on low heat for about an hour and 15 minutes
  13. While chicken stews, cook bacon lardons in small saute pan over medium heat until golden brown
  14. Remove bacon from pan, drain on paper towels
  15. Keep about 1 tablespoon of fat in the pan
  16. Saute mushrooms in the bacon fat until golden brown
  17. Set mushrooms aside
  18. In a small saucepan, combine pearl onions, the pinch of sugar, and two tablespoons of butter
  19. Add enough water to just cover the onions
  20. Cover pan with parchment paper, trimmed to the same size as the pan
  21. Bring the onions to a boil
  22. Reduce to a simmer, and cook until water has evaporated
  23. Remove paper cover and cook until onions are golden brown
  24. Set onions aside
  25. Add remaining cup of wine to the pan, scraping up the bits on the bottom of the pan
  26. Season with salt and pepper and reduce over medium-high heat until thick enough to coat the back of the spoon
  27. Once the chicken is cooked through, carefully remove from the liquid
  28. Cut the chicken into quarters, and arrange on deep serving platter
  29. Strain the liquid the chicken was cooking in into the reduced red wine
  30. Add the bacon, mushrooms, and pearl onions
  31. Season with salt and pepper to taste
  32. Mix in two tablespoons of butter
  33. Pour the sauce over the chicken and serve
